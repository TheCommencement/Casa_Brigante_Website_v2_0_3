When Love Was Enough

Being loved is not the same as being valued.

Kimberly has spent so much of her life surviving that she no longer remembers what it feels like to simply live. Between the weight of single motherhood, complicated relationships, and years of emotional scars, she’s convinced herself that settling is safer than hoping for something more.

But when an unexpected connection forces her to confront the difference between being loved and being valued, Kimberly begins a journey that challenges everything she thought she deserved. As old wounds resurface and the people closest to her are tested, she must decide whether healing means holding on... or finally finding the strength to let go.

A raw, emotionally charged novel about love, motherhood, resilience, and discovering that sometimes the greatest act of courage is believing you’re worthy of the life you’ve always wanted.
